# TRUE PURPOSE OF THIS SABBATICAL

This trip is NOT primarily about rest, luxury, or checking countries off a list.

It is about:

* building a peak-life era
* creating memories that still feel emotionally vivid decades later
* maximizing awe, emotional intensity, and aliveness
* experiencing radically different emotional worlds
* feeling deeply alive with my husband
* gathering inspiration for future life directions
* potentially discovering future career/creative inspiration
* building a temporary alternate life, not just traveling continuously

This trip should feel like:
“these were the years worth remembering.”

The goal is NOT constant comfort or balance.

The goal is:
* emotionally memorable contrast
* cinematic experiences
* surreal moments
* deep immersion
* psychologically distinct chapters
* unforgettable wildlife encounters
* temporary-life routines between periods of intensity

The ideal structure is:
* intense emotional peaks
* followed by intentional grounding and decompression

NOT:
* constant moderation
* nonstop movement
* or permanent relaxation.

---

# VERY IMPORTANT: FLEXIBILITY & SERENDIPITY

Do NOT over-optimize the route into rigidity.

This sabbatical must preserve:
* spontaneity
* emotional detours
* irrational decisions
* staying longer in places we unexpectedly love
* adjusting plans based on energy, weather, people, or emotional resonance

I want approximately 20–30% intentional flexibility in the itinerary.

Do NOT create a hyper-fragile route where every week is tightly optimized.

The trip should feel alive, adaptable, and emotionally responsive.

**Critical constraint:** Flexibility must operate WITHIN the confirmed geographic arc.
Detours that create regional backtracking (e.g., returning to NE Asia after entering SEA) inflate both cost and energy significantly and should be avoided even when emotionally tempting.
Flexibility means depth (staying longer in one place) — not directionality (reversing the route).

---

# VERY IMPORTANT: TEMPORARY-LIFE CHAPTERS

The slower chapters are NOT “slower tourism.”

They are intentional temporary-life periods.

**Duration:** Each chapter should contain 5–10 ACTIVE slow days.
Do NOT count activity days (snowboarding, trekking, diving, safaris) toward this total.
A 12-day Hakuba stay with 9 snowboard days has roughly 3 slow days — that is NOT a temporary-life chapter on its own.

**Confirmed temporary-life chapters:**
* Tokyo (Japan Autumn)
* Kyoto (Japan Autumn — temple routine)
* Hakuba (Japan Winter — snowboard rhythm + onsen; activity days excluded)
* Taipei (Taiwan)
* Penang (Malaysia — best café-culture slow chapter in SEA)
* Jeonju (South Korea — hanok village, Korean food, quiet)

**Bali is NOT a valid temporary-life chapter** — it is too touristic and curated to provide genuine grounding.
Use Nusa Penida, Jeonju, Kanazawa, Penang, or similar less-curated places instead.

During these chapters, optimize for:
* apartment stays over hotels
* grocery routines
* cooking
* recurring cafés
* structured mornings
* gym access
* meditation
* side projects / AI exploration
* language exposure (especially basic Mandarin)
* emotional integration
* routine and belonging

These chapters are essential for:
* resetting awe sensitivity
* avoiding emotional flattening
* nervous system recovery
* creative inspiration
* helping the trip feel like a lived life instead of continuous consumption

Potential emotional “home frequency” places should be taken seriously, especially:
* Taiwan
* Japan
* selected slower beach/mountain towns

# VERY IMPORTANT: GEOGRAPHIC EFFICIENCY

Geographic efficiency is a HARD CONSTRAINT, not just an optimization preference.

Regional backtracking (e.g., NE Asia → SEA → NE Asia → SEA) inflates:
* flight costs (real budget impact)
* logistical energy
* packing pressure
* emotional fatigue from transit

The confirmed geographic arc is:
**Turkey → South Asia (Nepal + India) → NE Asia (Japan + Korea + Taiwan + China) → SEA (Indonesia + Philippines + Singapore + Thailand + Malaysia) → home**

Every hop should move the route forward.
Do not reopen a region once the route has moved past it — even if a missed destination is compelling.

# VERY IMPORTANT: ENDING ARC

The final phase of the sabbatical should intentionally become more reflective and emotionally integrative.

Avoid ending the trip with nonstop escalation and peak intensity until the final day.

The ending should gradually create:
* reflection
* emotional integration
* calmer beauty
* slower pacing
* nostalgia
* meaning-making
* transition back into normal life

A softer ending is preferable to a permanently escalating one.

**Current confirmed ending: Komodo (June).**
Komodo is dramatic and elemental — dragons, remote islands, world-class diving — but it ends on raw natural beauty rather than urban intensity or escalating complexity. It is a strong emotional close: wild, elemental, and final. Not a peak that demands something bigger after it.
